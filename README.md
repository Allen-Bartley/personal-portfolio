You'll find that this portfolio is more than just a list of projectsâ€”it's the result of a decade-long commitment to building practical technical skills in an environment with limited local tech opportunities. Living in Boone County, West Virginia, meant that to pursue my passion for technology, I had to create my own learning experiences. This portfolio documents that hands-on journey.

### Turning Problems into Projects

Nearly every project documented here began as a solution to a real-world problem faced by me or my family. My home lab wasn't just for abstract learning; it was our production environment.

- **When my mother wanted to watch decades-old family videos**, it led to the [Analog Revival Hub](https://github.com/Allen-Bartley/analog-revival-hub), a project focused on digitizing VHS tapes and making them accessible through modern streaming devices.
- **When Wi-Fi dead zones plagued our inherited property**, it resulted in the [Residential Network Architecture](https://github.com/Allen-Bartley/residential-network-architecture) project, solving coverage issues with powerline adapters and enterprise equipment.
- **When a family laptop had Windows installation problems**, I developed the [Windows Installation Fix](https://github.com/Allen-Bartley/windows-corruption-recovery) using offline installation methods to preserve their data.

This philosophy of practical, user-focused problem-solving is the foundation of all my work.

### Dedication, Readiness, and the Road to Remote

For over a decade, my professional life involved a daily commute of up to 1.5 hours to the Charleston area for the best available on-site tech roles. For just as long, my ability to pursue remote work was limited by a bonded DSL connection with a download of 20Mbps and a upload speeds of just 1Mbps.

This environment shaped my approach: I had to be self-reliant, which is why these projects are solo endeavors. I had to be dedicated, and I had to find a way to keep growing. Now, with the recent arrival of gigabit fiber to my home, those constraints are gone.

I am actively seeking a fully remote role where I can bring this passion, resilience, and proven history of practical problem-solving to a collaborative team. With gigabit access and extensive hands-on experience, I'm ready to contribute to remote technical teams tackling real-world challenges.
